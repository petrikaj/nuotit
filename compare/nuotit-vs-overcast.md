## Audio Processing

Both apps care about audio quality — but they approach it very differently.

| Feature | Nuotit | Overcast |
|---------|:------:|:-------:|
| 9-Band Parametric EQ | ✅ | ❌ |
| Compressor | ✅ | ❌ |
| Peak Limiter | ✅ | ❌ |
| Expander | ✅ | ❌ |
| Voice Isolation | ✅ AI-powered | ❌ |
| Voice Boost | ❌ (use dynamics instead) | ✅ |
| Smart Speed | ❌ | ✅ |
| AI Audio Assist | ✅ | ❌ |
| Audio Profiles | ✅ Per show/environment | ❌ |
| Spectrum Analyzer | ✅ | ❌ |
| LUFS Metering | ✅ | ❌ |

**Overcast** gives you two powerful toggles — Voice Boost (levels out quiet voices) and Smart Speed (shortens silences). Simple and effective.

**Nuotit** gives you the full toolkit — EQ, compression, limiting, expansion, voice isolation, and AI assistance. More control, more precision, more scenarios covered.

## Who Should Choose Which?

### Choose **Nuotit** if you:
- Want full control over how your podcasts sound (EQ, dynamics, voice isolation)
- Encounter podcasts with poor audio quality regularly
- Create clips for social media with captions
- Need on-device transcripts with complete privacy
- Listen to local audio files

### Choose **Overcast** if you:
- Want simple, effective audio improvements (Voice Boost + Smart Speed)
- Value silence trimming to save time
- Prefer a minimal, text-focused UI
- Need Apple Watch support
