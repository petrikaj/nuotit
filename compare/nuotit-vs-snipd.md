# Nuotit vs. Snipd

> Snipd and Nuotit both use AI for audio — but they solve entirely different problems. Snipd extracts knowledge from what podcasts say. Nuotit shapes how your audio sounds and makes any file searchable, privately.

## What Snipd Does Well

Snipd is built for the knowledge-focused podcast listener. Its AI generates chapter summaries, identifies highlight moments, and creates shareable quote cards — then exports to Notion, Readwise, or Obsidian. If your goal is to capture, organize, and act on ideas from podcasts, Snipd is purpose-built for exactly that workflow.

Cross-platform availability (iOS and Android) is useful for listeners who switch devices. Feed-provided transcripts are displayed from Snipd's server-side processing.

---

## Where They Differ

### Two Different Kinds of AI

Snipd's AI focuses on *content* — what is being said, what is worth saving, how to export it to a knowledge tool.

Nuotit's AI focuses on *audio quality and transcription* — how to make the audio sound the way you want it, and how to make any file in your library searchable and navigable locally.

These are complementary rather than competing. A listener who uses Snipd for podcast knowledge capture and Nuotit for audiobooks, voice memos, and local music would get real value from both.

### Library and Local Files

Snipd doesn't position itself as a personal audio library — it's a knowledge capture tool built around podcast content. Library management and playlist features are limited compared to a dedicated player.

Nuotit's library is the core product: RSS subscriptions, imported music, audiobooks, voice memos, and lectures all treated equally, with advanced smart playlists, batch operations, and a persistent mini-player.

### On-Device vs. Server-Side Processing

Snipd's AI features (summaries, highlights, transcripts) are server-side. Audio is sent to Snipd's infrastructure for processing. An account is required to access AI features.

Nuotit generates transcripts on-device for any file on supported hardware (iPhone 15 Pro+ / M1 iPad+). Nothing is sent anywhere. No account required. This matters especially for private recordings — a voice memo or downloaded lecture can be transcribed with complete privacy.

### Audio Processing

Snipd focuses on the content of what's being said, with no manual audio processing tools.

Nuotit focuses on the listening experience itself. It provides a professional-grade audio toolkit: a 9-band parametric EQ, dynamics processing (compressor, limiter, expander), and custom per-show audio profiles, allowing you to tailor your sound for pristine playback across different environments.

### Clip Export

Snipd generates shareable video quote cards from AI highlights. These are not downloadable video files — they are share links.

Nuotit exports downloadable video clips with dynamic burned-in captions, animated waveforms, and custom templates in three social-ready formats (Story, Square, Landscape) — all generated on your device.

## Which One Fits?

### Snipd is a great fit if you

- Use podcasts primarily for research, learning, or professional development
- Want AI-generated chapter summaries and highlight extraction
- Export podcast insights to Notion, Readwise, or Obsidian
- Listen on both iOS and Android.

### Nuotit is a great fit if you

- Want a unified library for podcasts, audiobooks, voice memos, and local music
- Prefer on-device transcription — for any file, with complete privacy
- Want professional audio processing and per-show audio profiles
- Create video clips for social media with dynamic captions
- Value zero tracking, no account required, and no advertising.

### Consider both if you

- Listen to podcasts for both knowledge extraction (Snipd) and general enjoyment (Nuotit)
- Have a mix of RSS content, imported audiobooks, and private voice memos
- Want AI-powered content summaries from Snipd alongside private on-device transcription from Nuotit.
